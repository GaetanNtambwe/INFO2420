Web Site Critiques

Questions

  1. What was your first impression of this site?
  2. How does this site organize the flow of information?(Do they have links on the left and text on the right? some other scheme? NO scheme?)
  3. Does the site structure make sense or does it confuse you? Why?
  4. Does the site graphics/layout/multimedia enhance the site or detract from it? Why?
  5. How long did it take for the home page to load? Was the load time reasonable?
  6. Was the text color and size readable? If a background color or image was used, did it enhance or detract from the site?
  7. Did the site have a navigation bar or other navigation tool? Was the navigation well done?
  8. Did the site have a color scheme or look and feel that was repeated on all the pages?
  9. How does the site respond to smaller window sizes (responsive design)?
  10. What does this site offer that was unusual or very well done?

Web site: http://www.traderjoes.com/ Traderjoe's

My first impression of the Traderjoe's website is that there is too much information at once, which confused me a little at first. However ,I later found a list, or ribbon on the left that separated each section of the website for a better navigation

the site structure make sense to me,because the information shared on the website focuses on their products, discoveries, and recipes that people may be interested,organized from the most important to the least important. Regarding the graphics, the graphics used in the website, illustrated the meaning of their market, which is based on farms.

The load time is reasonable for me,and the text color and size are readable and reasonable.The background-color design allows for easy readibility of page.

About the navigation bar, the site include oone, and the tool is well done. The web page uses a consistent a color scheme accross different pages.

The site responds well to smaller window sizes. The elements resize and stack appropriately, making it usable on tablets and phones.

One unusualbut well done aspect of the site is that it intentionally avoids online shopping instead of supports in the store experience. Overall, the website look good to navigate
